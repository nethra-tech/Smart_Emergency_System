CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

CPR is an emergency procedure performed when someone's breathing or heartbeat has stopped. It combines chest compressions and rescue breaths to maintain circulation and oxygenation.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Check the person's responsiveness by tapping their shoulders and asking "Are you okay?"
  2. Call emergency services (112) or ask someone to call while you begin CPR
  3. Place the person on their back on a firm surface
  4. Kneel beside the person's chest and place the heel of one hand on the center of their chest (lower half of sternum)
  5. Place your other hand on top of the first hand, interlock your fingers
  6. Push hard and fast - at least 2 inches deep at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute
  7. After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths (if trained)
  8. Continue cycles of 30 compressions and 2 breaths until help arrives

Choking - Heimlich Maneuver

The Heimlich maneuver is a first aid procedure used to treat upper airway obstructions (choking) by foreign objects. It's crucial for saving lives during choking incidents.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Ask the person "Are you choking?" If they can't speak or cough, proceed
  2. Stand behind the person and wrap your arms around their waist
  3. Make a fist with one hand and place it just above their navel
  4. Grasp your fist with your other hand
  5. Perform quick, upward and inward thrusts
  6. Repeat thrusts until the object is expelled or the person becomes unconscious
  7. If unconscious, lay them down and begin CPR

Basic First Aid

Basic first aid skills are essential for treating minor injuries and stabilizing victims until professional help arrives. Knowing these techniques can save lives.

Essential First Aid Steps:

  1. Ensure safety - Check the scene for dangers before approaching
  2. Assess consciousness - Check if the person is responsive
  3. Call for help - Dial 112 or ask someone to call
  4. Check breathing and pulse
  5. Position the person appropriately based on their condition
  6. Control bleeding with direct pressure
  7. Treat for shock - Keep them warm and calm
  8. Monitor vital signs until help arrives

Burn Treatment

Burns require immediate first aid to prevent further tissue damage and reduce pain. Proper initial treatment can significantly improve healing outcomes.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Remove the person from the source of the burn
  2. Cool the burn under cool (not cold) running water for at least 10-20 minutes
  3. Remove any jewelry, watches, or tight clothing near the burned area
  4. Do NOT apply ice, butter, or toothpaste to burns
  5. Cover the burn with a sterile, non-stick bandage or clean cloth
  6. Take over-the-counter pain relievers if needed
  7. Seek medical attention for severe burns (blistering, large area, face, hands, feet)

Controlling Bleeding

Controlling severe bleeding is crucial in emergencies. Acting quickly can prevent shock and save lives. Always use personal protective equipment when available.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Protect yourself - Use gloves if available
  2. Apply direct pressure to the wound using a clean cloth or bandage
  3. Maintain pressure for at least 10-15 minutes without checking
  4. If blood soaks through, add more layers - do NOT remove the first one
  5. Elevate the injured area above heart level if possible
  6. Apply a tourniquet only as a last resort for life-threatening bleeding
  7. Monitor for signs of shock and keep the person calm
